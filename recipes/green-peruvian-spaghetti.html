Green Peruvian Spaghetti

Description

Yummy delicous plate of a flavorful pesto green spaghetti. The spaghetti can eaten on its own or as a side preferrably with a fried breaded steak. The spaghetti's main ingredients is pasta basil, oil, garlic, feta, salt and pepper. No variety of nuts are used in this pesto spaghetti much different than it's Italian counterpart.

This dish is so easy to make my grandma blindfolded while attending a baseball game make it. Unlike other lame Italian pestos this brilliant Peruvian pesto is made from blending rather than through a food proccessor making a smooth lovely velvety pesto that cause you water at the mouth. From blending you will not have bits of basil leaves. Instead your plate will lathered with a beautiful green sauce that is loaded with flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of uncooked spaghetti
  • 5 ounces of fresh baby spinach
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 2 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 1 shallot chopped
  • 4 ounces feta cheese
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1/4 cup olive oil

Steps

  1. Cook spaghetti according to packaged directions. Drain spaghetti reserving 1/4 cup of the water
  2. Add the spinach, basil, garlic, shallot, feta cheese, salt, pepper to a blender and blend into a sauce
  3. Transfer the sauce into a large mixing bowl and stir in the olive oil. Season sauce with additional salt & pepper if needed
  4. Add the cooked spaghetti to the bowl and toss with the sauce until all the pasta is coated. Stir in a little bit of the reserved pasta water if needed to reach desired consistency
